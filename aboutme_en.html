About Me

A Story of Passion for Learning, Creation, and Innovation

From an early age, the world of algorithms and data patterns has captivated me like an endless intellectual adventure — one that reveals ever-deeper layers of elegance and possibility.

Dear Visitor,

I am Ali-Asghar (Arta) Razazpour, a Bachelor of Science graduate in Computer Engineering from the University of Bojnurd, Iran (February 2026). This fascination led me to one of the most exciting frontiers of modern science: Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning; where human creativity meets computational power to answer age-old questions and raise new ones.

Throughout my undergraduate studies, I chose the path of hands-on, independent research. These experiences not only sharpened my technical skills but also taught me resilience in the face of hard problems, the art of translating creative ideas into robust code, and the discipline of rigorous scientific evaluation.

Research Journey

Key Projects and Achievements

Large Language Model Fine-tuning

My bachelor's thesis involved fine-tuning LLMs (Llama) using LoRA to simulate the linguistic patterns of a narcissistic personality. This project immersed me in NLP, representation learning, and affective computing.

Bio-Inspired Algorithms

Designed a novel, deterministic algorithm for the N-Queens problem inspired by DNA base-pairing rules. This method demonstrated superior speed and reliability compared to standard genetic algorithms for N ≤ 20.

Sports Data Analytics

Implemented an end-to-end machine learning pipeline for predicting football match outcomes using over 20 years of historical data and ensemble techniques like Random Forest and XGBoost.
